I am a **Principal Data Steward** at [Novo Nordisk](https://www.novonordisk.com), Denmark, where I lead teams focused on making life science data **Findable, Accessible, Interoperable, and Reusable** (FAIR).

## What I Do

I work at the intersection of scientific computing, data management, and engineering — building the infrastructure that enables researchers to find, access, and reuse experimental data at scale.

- **FAIR Data & Open Science** — designing metadata standards, data catalogs, and automated ingestion pipelines for experimental data
- **Research Data Management** — leading development of data management platforms including [SciCat](https://github.com/SciCatProject), an open-source scientific data catalog
- **High-Performance Computing** — years of experience with MPI-parallel simulation codes for astrophysical phenomena
- **Team Leadership** — building and mentoring cross-functional engineering teams across research and IT

## Background

My career spans scientific research and software engineering. I hold a PhD in astrophysics and have worked at institutions including the [European Spallation Source (ESS)](https://europeanspallationsource.se), the Technical University of Denmark (DTU), and Novo Nordisk.

Earlier in my career I developed MHD simulation codes for modelling jets and supernovae, and FPGA-based digital signal processing filters. I now apply that same rigour to data engineering challenges in the life sciences.
